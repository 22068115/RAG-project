Overview
This subject covers basic concepts of data centric thinking. The main areas discussed are; Populations and Samples; Sampling concepts; Types of Data; Descriptive Methods; Estimation and Inference; Modelling. The subject takes a computational and nonparametric approach, before briefly discussing theoretical concepts and distribution theory in technical and lay language.

Learning Outcomes
Collapse all
Upon completion of this subject, graduates will be able to:
1.
Describe different types of data with reference to real-world examples using descriptive statistics and graphical methods.

keyboard_arrow_down
2.
Identify data collection strategies considering populations and samples to provide unbiased and reliable data.

keyboard_arrow_down
3.
Estimate population parameters in R, using non-parametric and Normal theory methods to draw valid conclusions from data.

keyboard_arrow_down
4.
Examine relationships between variables using correlation and simple linear regression, including assessment of model fit, assumptions, and statistical significance, to interpret and communicate data-driven insights.
